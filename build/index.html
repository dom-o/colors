This is a tool for finding distinct colors/groups of colors.

I made this for my team when I was head routesetter at a climbing wall. We had climbs on the wall, and we marked which holds belonged to each climb with colored tape. Sometimes one of the routesetters would mark two adjacent climbs with colors that were hard to tell apart.

Using this tool, I picked all the tape colors we had, and I said, "Well, we mark every climb with two colors, a main and a tail, and I figure the main color is more important for distinction than the tail color." So I picked groups of size 2, and I weighted the first color more than the second. Then I had a list of the tape colors that were the most distinct from each other, and as long as we stuck to those colors, we knew it would be easy to tell climbs apart.

Pick some colors.

I calculate the difference between groups as the average of the color difference between each color, that is, for two groups of three colors, I find the difference between the two first colors of each group, the difference between the two second colors, and the difference between the two third colors, and average them.

You can weight the average with these sliders. The further right you slide, the more important the difference between colors in that position will be to the overall difference. For groups of size one, the weight doesn't matter, and if you don't want to weight the results at all, leave the sliders alone or slide all of them all the way to one direction.
